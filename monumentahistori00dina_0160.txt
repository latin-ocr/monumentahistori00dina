ERATOSTHENES

Eratosthenes was librarian at Alexandria Rc.
247-222. He made a Greek translation of Himilco's
work (v. supra, pp. 9—13)‚ and also commented on the
works of Pytheas, in whom he had much confidence.
His only knowledge of the Celts was derived from
these writers and has been already dealt with.

Celtic names are respectively Cernunnos and Smertullos: cf. Corpus Inscrip-
tionum Latinorum, xiii. p. 467, No. 3026 c. These gods appear in old Irish
literature: vide Whitney Stokes, in Revue Geltique, iii. p. 175. Also see
Arbois de Jubainvffle, Gours de litterature celtique, v. pp. 347-353, and
